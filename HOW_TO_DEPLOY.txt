==================================================
POLYSCAN — HOW TO DEPLOY (FREE, 10 MINUTES)
==================================================

You need two free accounts:
1. GitHub — github.com (store your files)
2. Render  — render.com (host your app online)

==================================================
STEP 1 — UPLOAD TO GITHUB
==================================================

1. Go to github.com and sign in (or create free account)
2. Click the green "New" button to create a repository
3. Name it: polyscan
4. Click "Create repository"
5. On the next page click "uploading an existing file"
6. Drag and drop ALL files in this folder:
   - server.js
   - package.json
   - render.yaml
   - the "public" folder (contains index.html)
7. Click "Commit changes"

==================================================
STEP 2 — DEPLOY ON RENDER
==================================================

1. Go to render.com and sign in with GitHub
2. Click "New +" then "Web Service"
3. Connect your GitHub account if asked
4. Select your "polyscan" repository
5. Render will auto-detect the settings
6. Make sure these are set:
   - Build Command: npm install
   - Start Command: node server.js
   - Plan: Free
7. Click "Create Web Service"
8. Wait 2-3 minutes while it builds

==================================================
STEP 3 — YOUR PERMANENT LINK
==================================================

Once deployed Render gives you a link like:
  https://polyscan.onrender.com

That is your permanent scanner link.
- Open it on any browser on your laptop
- Open it on your TV browser
- Bookmark it on your phone
- It runs 24/7 with no Claude needed

==================================================
IMPORTANT NOTES
==================================================

- Free Render plan "sleeps" after 15 min of no activity
  To keep it always awake, use uptimerobot.com (free)
  Set it to ping your Render URL every 5 minutes

- Weather data refreshes every 10 minutes automatically

- The scanner reads exact Wunderground stations
  (same sources Polymarket uses for resolution)
